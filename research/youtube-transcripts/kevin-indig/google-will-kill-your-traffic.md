## Overview

This is a conversation between Ahrefs CEO Tim Soulo and Kevin Indig covering two directions of AI's impact on SEO:

1. How AI is changing search itself (AI overviews, Google AI Mode, LLMs)
2. How to use AI as a tool to do SEO and marketing better

---

## Part 1: How AI Is Changing Search

### The "Crocodile Chart" — Impressions Up, Clicks Down

Kevin's view: **2024 was peak traffic year.** He believes we'll look back at 2024 as the highest traffic point we've ever seen — and he's intentionally not saying "organic traffic," he means traffic as a whole.

The impression increase is mostly due to how Google counts impressions for AI overviews. Every URL cited in an AI overview automatically gets an impression once the overview loads, even before a user expands it. Kevin's study found that over 85% of people click "load more" on AI overviews to see the full answer — but reading behavior follows the same pattern as classic search: most attention goes to the upper third.

**Key implication:** Being cited early in an AI overview matters most.

### Is Anyone Immune?

Kevin hasn't seen anyone immune to the traffic decline. By business type:

- **Publishers:** Not in a good place.
- **Affiliate SEO:** Struggling. Kevin notes affiliate SEO was "such a cheat code" for the past five years, and that era is ending.
- **SaaS companies:** Revenue relatively flat to up. Some are actually seeing *higher* revenue after AI overviews launched because people get better answers faster and reach a conversion point much quicker.
- **E-commerce:** Not a huge impact from AI overviews yet.
- **Consumer/B2B marketplaces:** Revenue slightly down, but not to the same degree as traffic loss.

### Traffic Down, Revenue Flat — Why?

Two possible explanations, and Kevin thinks both are true:

1. Top-of-funnel informational content had more downstream pipeline impact than we realized — we just couldn't attribute it.
2. AI overviews are appearing for more commercial and transactional queries than current measurement tools can capture, because people are searching with longer, more specific prompts.

Some SaaS companies are seeing *higher revenue* after AI overviews because users get better answers faster and convert faster. The companies struggling are those that benefited from sheer page volume in the old SEO world — that advantage doesn't translate to LLMs the same way.

### Are AI Overviews Showing Up for Commercial Queries?

As of early 2024, studies showed 99.9% of AI overview keywords were informational. But starting March 2024 (when Google launched a core update and significantly dialed up AI overviews), Kevin started seeing them appear for comparison queries and "where to buy" type searches. His view: AI overviews are cutting deeper into commercial and transactional intent than we can currently measure.

## Part 2: How to Show Up in AI Overviews

The core answer: **give the most relevant and best answer.**

This is easier for specific, long-tail prompts than for short-head keywords. "Best SEO tool" is vague — hard to know what the best answer looks like. "Best rank tracker for a five-person company in the legal space under $200/month" is much more specific — easier to create content that clearly answers it.

Three main ingredients for AI overview visibility:

1. **Content freshness** — how recently updated and current is your content?
2. **Relevance** — how closely does your content match what people are actually prompting?
3. **Brand authority and trust** — have people heard of you? What sentiment exists around your brand?

Kevin's takeaway: these are eerily similar to classic SEO first principles. The core tactics apply — AI just accelerates them and makes being good at them more important.

## Part 3: The Bigger Shift — It's Not Just Your Website Anymore

Kevin's study (conducted with Eric Vanbuskerk, 70 participants, 30 hours of video, 125,000 words of transcript) revealed a key behavioral insight:

**People generally like and believe AI overviews — and they validate those AI answers on Reddit, YouTube, and other forums.**

It's almost a verification check: "This seems true, but I'd rather see if another human or unbiased source says the same thing." Those sources include Reddit, YouTube, forums, Trustpilot, G2, and Google Maps reviews.

**The implication:** You need to care more about what *other websites* say about you, not just your own site. This was always true for SEO (links, mentions, digital PR), but now it's even more critical because:

- Different LLMs pull from different platforms (Google/Gemini leans on Reddit; ChatGPT leans more on Wikipedia)
- You need to think about how you're represented on each of those platforms
- Classic SEO was about your website — AI search is multi-platform

Kevin's framing: "SEO has become much more multi-channel or multi-platform — and way beyond just mentions and backlinks, in the sense that there's a different mix of sources that LLMs are pulling from."

## Part 4: Is SEO Dead?

Kevin's view: **SEO died many times and revitalized each time.**

But the more important framing is what's happening in the minds of leadership (CMOs, CEOs, CROs). They see a 30% structural decline in web traffic and SEO looks like it might be in a coma — unless you can show revenue data that proves otherwise.

What he's seeing across clients and larger companies:

- Leadership sees traditional SEO declining
- Everyone is asking: what comes after SEO?
- The answer they're landing on: AI/LLM visibility
- Budgets are opening up to invest in AI search visibility
- There's a perceived first-mover advantage — and in some cases, it's real

**The first-mover window:** Kevin estimates it's open for another 6–18 months before investors expect to see returns. AI traffic currently converts at a much higher rate than organic traffic, but it's still a small percentage of total revenue (roughly 1–10% of the organic bottom line for most companies). The bet is that AI traffic grows to meaningful scale.

He's seen for the first time a chart where a company's Google SEO revenue line and ChatGPT revenue line crossed in the middle — forming an X. Early signal, small company, but it's happening.

## Part 5: Google AI Overviews vs. ChatGPT — Are They Different?

Yes, significantly different.

LLMs do lean on search engines (Bing, Google) to ground their answers, so there's a carryover effect from strong Google SEO. But the winners on Google are not always the winners on ChatGPT:

- **Consolidated markets** (e.g., CRM software — dominated by Salesforce and HubSpot): Same winners on Google and ChatGPT.
- **Fragmented markets** (e.g., e-commerce, some SaaS categories): Completely different winners. Reasons include: some sites accidentally block LLMs, some have old-school SEO approaches that don't translate, and there's genuine room for challengers.

**Kevin's minimum recommendation:** Every company should check whether they dominate their market on ChatGPT the same way they do on Google — because it's not always true. And tools already exist to reverse-engineer why competitors are more or less visible in LLMs (specific content gaps, data/stats they have that you don't, brand reputation and sentiment differences).

## Part 6: Top AI Use Cases for SEO and Marketing

### Use Case 1 — Analyze Sales Calls at Scale

While working with Ramp (fintech company that raised at a $16B valuation), Kevin's team:

1. Took transcripts of conversations between prospects who converted to customers and the sales team
2. Used an AI tool (Humata AI) to analyze 50+ transcripts
3. Asked questions like: What problems are customers facing? What solutions are they currently using? Who else are they evaluating? What does success look like?
4. Used those insights to create blog content that converted at a much higher rate

**Why it works:** You're identifying what your target audience actually cares about from first principles — not from search volume data. The content you create covers topics that nobody else is targeting, making you the only source of knowledge for those questions. Competitors looking at your site with SEO tools will be confused about why you're targeting low-volume topics — while you're watching traffic and conversions go up.

**Key filter:** Only analyze conversations from prospects who *became* customers (not all prospects). That ensures you're learning from the conversations where persuasion actually worked.

### Use Case 2 — Vibe Coding Interactive Tools

LLMs and tools like v0, Lovable, and Cursor have made it possible for non-technical marketers to build proof-of-concept tools: calculators, generators, quizzes, widgets, mini apps.

**Why it matters for SEO:**

- Interactive tools are massive backlink magnets
- They improve content engagement and time on page
- They can improve conversion rates when embedded in blog content or content hubs
- They can be embedded across many pages, not just on a single landing page

Kevin's approach: marketers build a proof of concept to validate the idea works, then hand it off to an actual developer to build it production-ready. The marketing team doesn't need to ship production code — they need to prove the concept.

**Ahrefs' approach:** They now have a dedicated developer embedded in the marketing team specifically to vibe code marketing ideas. The reason: even with AI coding tools, developers are dramatically more efficient than non-developers (they don't get stuck at the last 1%). The goal is turning marketing ideas into proof-of-concepts quickly, then pitching them to the product team for productization.

**Caveat:** Vibe coding can be extremely frustrating — you get to 99% quickly and the last 1% takes five times as long. Having even basic coding knowledge (understanding fundamentals, being able to talk to AI "as a developer to developer") makes a significant difference in efficiency.

## Key Takeaways

- 2024 was likely peak traffic year. Clicks are going down structurally.
- Revenue impact is lower than traffic impact for most SaaS companies — some are actually seeing higher revenue.
- AI search isn't killing SEO. It's creating a new cycle with a first-mover window.
- The core SEO first principles still apply — AI accelerates them and makes them more important.
- The biggest change is user behavior: people are searching with longer, more specific prompts, and a vacuum exists for hyper-specific content targeting those prompts.
- You need to care about what other platforms say about you (Reddit, Wikipedia, G2, Trustpilot) — not just your own website.
- Different LLMs pull from different source mixes. Google/Gemini favors Reddit. ChatGPT leans more on Wikipedia.
- Check your ChatGPT visibility now — especially in fragmented markets, Google winners ≠ ChatGPT winners.
- Best AI use cases: analyzing sales conversations at scale to find content topics, and vibe coding interactive tools as backlink magnets and conversion drivers.
