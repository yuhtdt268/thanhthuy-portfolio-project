## Core Argument

The old content playbook is broken. Series B companies hired content leads, trained leadership to believe traffic was the ultimate goal, spent enormous budgets on bloated topic clusters, and produced content nobody read. Brands were bragging about publishing 100 to 1,000 pieces per year with no readers.

The new reality: AI doesn't make you faster. It equips you to take on more work — work you probably wouldn't have done before. The promise of AI is scale. The fear is losing quality. Finding the balance between the two is the real challenge.

**Chima's prediction:** In four to five years, all AI search and Google's ecosystem will be designed to keep people inside their own platforms. Fighting to extract traffic from them is a losing battle. The only way to survive is to create demand-led content that pulls people directly into your own ecosystem.

## Part 1: How to Find Demand

Chima's approach to demand is simple and deliberately low-tech:

- **LinkedIn conversations** — what problems are people actively discussing?
- **Customer feedback from beta product launches** — what are real users struggling with?
- **Community events and conferences** — what questions come up in rooms of practitioners?
- **Subreddits** — what are people genuinely asking and debating?
- **Product roadmaps and emerging trends** — what problems are products being built to solve?

Half her time is no longer spent on keyword research. Demand signals come from listening to where your audience spends time — not from SEO tools.

## Part 2: The Content Types That Work

Chima's lean content mix — the only formats she invests in:

1. **Webinars** — highest ROI, massively underused
2. **Thought leadership content** — first-person, experience-driven
3. **Email** — most reliable distribution channel
4. **Research-led content** — original data analysis
5. **Product-led content** — content with a natural connection to the product

## Part 3: The Webinar Flywheel

Webinars are Chima's primary content engine. When she joined Moz in 2023, she launched a webinar program and learned through trial and error what works:

**Mistakes she made (and fixed):**

- Putting her own face on promotional assets instead of platforming the speaker → numbers jumped when she removed herself
- Relying on social media promotion instead of email → numbers jumped when she switched to email-first promotion
- Planning topics months in advance → now she reacts quickly to current demand signals
- Running weekly webinars → unsustainable, absolute nightmare to manage
- Not repurposing webinar content → "all the content we have in the graveyard absolutely breaks my heart"

**Webinar topics that perform:**

- Training on industry conversations happening right now
- Seasonal content tied to what's relevant
- How-to content tied to product workflows and launches

**The webinar flywheel:**

1. Identify demand signal
2. Find a thought leader already creating content around that topic
3. Use qualifying questions to segment registrations
4. Repurpose the webinar as a blog post
5. Chop into short-form content for social
6. Turn into downloadable assets
7. Remarket to leads via email sequence

**Real result:** One webinar got 1,738 sign-ups — mostly powered by email. Before adding storytelling to the email sequence, sign-ups were 187–230. After: thousands.

## Part 4: AI Workflow for Webinar Content

**Writing webinar landing pages with AI:**

1. Give it an example of copy you like
2. Tell it what you liked about the example
3. Explain how you want the copy structured
4. Provide all the information needed to write the page using the example as a template

**Email sequence from landing page copy:**

1. Take the landing page copy
2. Feed it into an email sequence GPT
3. Ask for a strategy for the first four emails
4. Always feed it a sample email you've liked in the past
5. Provide as much context as possible — you can never give LLMs too much context

**The most important element in email copy:** Fear. It's the most powerful emotion in a writer's toolkit. The goal is to keep pressing on the pain until the reader feels they absolutely need your solution.

**What to add to every AI output:**

- Storytelling (non-negotiable)
- First-person perspective
- Highlight the biggest fear immediately
- Show what happens if they don't take action
- Expose the false hopes they may be holding onto

## Part 5: Writing Informational Content with AI

**The old process (broken):**
Keyword research → content brief → copy the SERPs → include entities → check content score → optimize → publish. Completely robotic. Content looked like a Wikipedia page. Just regurgitating what already existed.

**The mistakes in early AI-assisted content:**

- Convoluted introductions
- Too many subheadings
- No workflows — just walls of text
- No multimodal assets
- Regurgitating information from the SERPs — same as everyone else

**The new process:**

1. Understand demand first
2. See what competitors have said to set a benchmark
3. Create an outline based on your own experience with the problem — not based on the SERP
4. Write from first person, add personal experiences
5. Provide resources to make it actionable
6. Add SME insight and multimodal assets
7. Distribute everywhere

**Real example:** An article on SEO topic clusters — produced 10 content assets from one piece. Still drives significant traffic to Moz's website. Not even ranking on Google. Traffic comes from demand, not from rankings.

**The Moz workflow for informational content:**

1. Go to Moz Content Brief tool, generate brief with target keyword
2. Feed the brief into a Content Assistant GPT
3. Ask it to analyze existing content already ranking for the topic
4. Combine the GPT's analysis with the Moz Content Brief for a super-detailed final brief
5. Write the article using that combined brief

**What all good informational thought leadership must have:**

- Storytelling (non-negotiable)
- First-person perspective
- How-to elements
- Downloadable assets
- Multimodal formats
- A natural connection to the product

## Part 6: Research-Led Content with AI

Chima used to avoid data analysis entirely because she hated math. LLMs changed that. Her process for turning large datasets into research-led content:

**Step 1 — Get the data into ChatGPT**
Ask it to read the file and return the data points.

**Step 2 — Get an overview that shapes the story**
Ask for a structured overview of what the data shows.

**Step 3 — Ask the most important question**
*"What question should I be asking if I were to write a blog post that turns this data into research findings?"*
This frames the entire piece around a narrative, not just numbers.

**Tools she uses for data analysis:**

- **Julius AI** — provides Python scripts she can review, edit, and run; does analysis in text mode
- **Google Colab** — for verifying results: she takes the Julius Python script, runs it in Colab against the original dataset to confirm the numbers match
- **ChatGPT** — as a backup to generate Python scripts for verification

**Real example:** Analyzed AI mode citation data — 95.69% citation rate according to Julius AI, 95.7% according to ChatGPT. Cross-verified in Google Colab. Then used a Content Assistant GPT to write the blog post from the verified findings.

## Part 7: The Full Demand-Led Content Playbook

1. **Understand demand** — go where your audience spends time (LinkedIn, Substack, conferences, communities)
2. **Create a content strategy based on that demand** — not based on keyword tools
3. **Use AI to support execution** — the keyword is *support*, not to do the execution
4. **Repurpose for multimodal** — video, podcast, audio, written
5. **Distribute everywhere**

## Key Takeaways

- **AI doesn't make you faster — it makes you capable of more work.** Be honest about this with leadership.
- **Demand signals come from communities, not keyword tools.** LinkedIn, Reddit, conferences, customer feedback.
- **Webinars are the most underused content format.** One webinar = landing page + blog post + short-form social + downloadable asset + email sequence.
- **Storytelling is non-negotiable.** It's what separates content that performs from content that ranks. You cannot teach an LLM storytelling — it has to come from the writer.
- **Research-led content is now accessible to non-technical marketers.** Julius AI + Google Colab makes large dataset analysis doable without knowing Python.
- **Stop writing for Google. Write for demand.** The piece on SEO topic clusters that drives Chima's best traffic doesn't even rank on Google — it drives traffic because it answers real demand.
- **The content-to-lead pipeline is real.** A repurposed blog post from a webinar brought in 1,400 leads. Even 2% conversion from 3,000 webinar leads = 60 new customers.
