# Article Analysis: AEO & SEO on Brand Terms Needs Brand Efforts

**Author**: Eli Schwartz
**Date Published**: Jun 11, 2026
**Source**: Product-Led SEO Newsletter

---

## The Common Misconception in SEO

There is a frequent conversation in the SEO industry where company leaders (CEOs, CMOs, Directors) demand their website rank #1 for comparison keywords like *"best CMS"*, *"WordPress vs Contently"*, or *"SurveyMonkey alternative"*. 

When they don't rank, they often:

- Request technical audits, backlink research, and page speed analysis.
- Blame their current SEO agency for poor performance.
- Push SEO managers to urgently sign on to new AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) tools.

---

## Newsflash: It’s Not a Technical Issue

Ranking for brand comparisons and "best-of" keywords is **not a technical SEO challenge**. 

- The top-ranking URLs are not winning because they nailed their URL structure, schema, or page speed. 
- They win because the underlying facts about their product are well-known, widely cited, and repeatedly validated by other sites. 
- You can have the best product on the internet and still get destroyed on a keyword by a competitor whose product is simply talked about more.
- SEO teams and agencies need to push back against leadership requests that try to solve brand reputation issues with technical SEO fixes.

---

## Brand Matters More Than Technical SEO

For brand-type queries, the most important factor is the **"fit" and reputation of the brand**.

- **Search Engine Goal**: When someone searches *"monday.com vs Asana"*, Google isn't looking for a page with both brand names in the title tag. Google wants to provide the most trustworthy and useful answer for a real buyer making a software decision.
- **Reputation Signals**: Trustworthiness is almost entirely downstream of reputation:
  - Who links to you.
  - How often you get mentioned in editorial contexts.
  - What comparison sites and review platforms say about you.
  - What real journalists write when they cover your category.
- **Zero Technical Impact**: None of these trust signals are impacted in the slightest by technical SEO.
- **The AI Overview Factor**: These pages still matter immensely even as AI Overviews expand. A buyer making a genuine product decision will not trust a brief AI summary; they want the full picture and will click through to find it.

---

## The Solution: Strategic PR & Communications

To win these queries, companies need a general PR-type effort—but not traditional press releases announcing funding rounds.

- **Strategic Seeding**: You must actively seed the facts and real differentiators about your product in places that matter (third-party reviews, social media, YouTube, Reddit).
- **Cross-Department Collaboration**: Brand awareness cannot be a separate track. The SEO team (promoting technical ideas) and the PR team (pitching stories) must work together. Brand teams should ultimately own AEO, but they must collaborate closely with SEO.

---

## The Content Creation Reality (And The Honesty Bar)

Before PR can even start, the content must actually exist. Shockingly, companies often wonder why they don't rank for terms they have never even published on their site.

Even when the page is written, it usually fails because it lacks objectivity:

- **The Biased Marketing Trap**: Most companies write "X vs Y" pages that declare themselves the winner in every category, refusing to acknowledge any tradeoffs. 
- **The Sophistication Filter**: Sophisticated buyers—and AI that imitates human thought—see through this biased marketing copy immediately.
- **The Honest Approach**: Pages that consistently rank share an uncomfortable honesty. They outline specific scenarios in which a competitor is actually the better choice, grounded in actual product behavior rather than positioning language.
- **Why Media Sites Win**: Creating honest content requires a product marketing team willing to document its own weaknesses and a content team with the autonomy to publish non-sales material. Most companies cannot clear this bar, which is why unbiased media sites dominate these search results.

---

## Pages Need Continuous Updates

Building a winning page is ultimately a **product problem**, not a keyword problem. If your product doesn't deserve to win the query, technical SEO and earned links will not save it.

- **The Stale Content Problem**: You cannot build a comparison page today, spend six months earning mentions and citations, and ignore the content. By the time your reputation signals are strong enough, the content is stale.
- **Parallel Execution**: Content updates and off-page PR work must move in parallel. This requires dedicated coordination; it must be someone's actual job, not a side project for the SEO team.

> **Core Takeaway**: If you want to rank as the "best" or have an honest comparison, you first need to write the objective content. And then you need to actually earn the reputation to back it up.
