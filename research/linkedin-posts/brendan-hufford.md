## Post

Google just published an official guide saying AEO and GEO are "still SEO."

They also said you don't need `llms.txt` files, chunking, or AI-specific schema. Just keep doing SEO.

Respectfully — I don't believe them. And neither should you.

Google has a consistent pattern of saying one thing publicly while their actual systems reward the opposite. We've seen this before:

**Example 1 — Chrome data and rankings**
Google publicly said Chrome data wasn't used in ranking websites. The antitrust trial confirmed it is a direct ranking signal.

**Example 2 — Link building**
Google publicly said to "avoid link building." The antitrust trial explicitly confirmed it's a key quality signal and that anchor text is a "valuable clue" for relevance.

Google's public advice always protects their own interests — not yours.

I've been tracking 100+ AEO prompts per client engagement for over a year. What I'm seeing in the data doesn't match what Google is telling us.

Does this mean ignore everything they say? No. But treat their guidance as one data point, not gospel.

**The bottom line:** Great content marketing means you can win in both SEO *and* AEO. You just need the right strategy behind it.
