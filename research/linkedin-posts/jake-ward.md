## Post

Too many companies are treating AI search as a completely different channel. They're building separate strategies, hiring separate teams, and creating separate content.

If you're already doing SEO, do this instead:

1. Open ChatGPT (free) or Mentions (paid)
2. Enter prompts you want to appear in
3. Study which brands AI recommends
4. Export the list of cited sources
5. Find where you're mentioned — and where you aren't
6. Prioritize by the most-cited sources

Then focus on increasing your presence across those same sources and topics.

**Specific tactics:**

- Get added to sources you're currently missing from
- Move higher in lists you're already on
- Create content based on topics that are currently being cited
- Publish on sites like Medium and LinkedIn Pulse
- Add natural mentions in relevant Reddit threads

This isn't AEO. It's earned media, owned content, and intentional distribution — guided by what AI is already rewarding.

AI search needs to be treated as an expansion of SEO, not a completely different channel.

## Key Visual: What People Think vs. What Actually Works

The post includes a diagram contrasting myths vs. reality in AI search visibility:

**What people think gets them recommended (myths):**

- AEO hacks
- llms.txt
- .md pages
- Schema
- GEO services
- Writing for AI

**What actually gets them recommended (reality):**

- SEO
- Reviews
- Entity consistency
- Owned content
- Earned media
- Topical authority
- YouTube videos
- Google rankings
- Industry publications
- Community threads
- Brand mentions
- Comparison pages
- Third-party recommendations
- Backlinks

The diagram visually sizes each factor by importance — SEO, topical authority, earned media, brand mentions, Google rankings, and backlinks are the largest circles. Technical AEO tactics (llms.txt, schema, .md pages) are shown as small, hollow circles with no weight.
